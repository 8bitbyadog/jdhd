# ADHD Quest: User Guide

Welcome to ADHD Quest, your JRPG-style productivity companion designed specifically for people with ADHD and creative workflows. This guide will help you get the most out of the app.

## Getting Started

ADHD Quest has three main sections:
- **Tasks** (Quest Log): Manage your to-do items
- **Time** (Time Crystal): Track your focus and break sessions
- **Creative** (Idea Inventory): Capture and develop creative ideas

You can switch between these sections using the menu at the top of the app.

## Tasks (Quest Log)

### Adding a New Task
1. Enter your task in the text field at the top of the Quest Log
2. Select a priority level (High, Medium, Low)
3. Choose a category (General, Creative, Work, Personal)
4. Click "Add Quest" to add it to your list

### Managing Tasks
- Click the checkbox to mark a task as complete
- Click the × button to delete a task
- Tasks are color-coded by priority:
  - Red: High priority
  - Orange: Medium priority
  - Green: Low priority

## Time (Time Crystal)

### Using the Timer
1. The timer defaults to Focus Time mode
2. Click "Start" to begin a focus session
3. The timer will count down and show your progress
4. When the timer completes, it will automatically switch to Break Time
5. You can manually switch between Focus and Break using the "Switch to..." button

### Timer Controls
- **Start**: Begin the timer
- **Pause**: Temporarily stop the timer
- **Reset**: Reset the timer to the beginning
- **Switch to...**: Change between Focus and Break modes

### Time Log
The Time Log section shows your recent completed time blocks, helping you track your productivity patterns.

## Creative (Idea Inventory)

### Capturing Ideas
1. Enter your creative idea in the text area
2. Select a category (General, Writing, Art, Music, Project)
3. Click "Save Idea" to add it to your inventory

### Using Random Prompts
1. Click "Random Prompt" to generate a creative prompt
2. If you like the prompt, click "Use This" to add it to your idea text
3. Or click "Dismiss" to close the prompt

### Managing Ideas
- Click the + button on an idea card to expand it and see the full text
- Click the × button to delete an idea
- Ideas are organized in a card layout for visual browsing

## Settings

### Data Management
Since ADHD Quest doesn't require an account, your data is stored locally in your browser. To prevent data loss:

1. Go to the Settings tab
2. Use "Export Data" to save a backup of your data
3. Use "Import Data" to restore from a backup

This is especially important if you:
- Clear your browser data
- Switch to a different device
- Want to transfer your data

## Tips for ADHD Users

### Managing Focus
- Use the Time Crystal for structured focus sessions
- Start with shorter focus periods (15-20 minutes) and gradually increase
- Take regular breaks to prevent burnout

### Reducing Overwhelm
- Add tasks to the Quest Log as soon as you think of them
- Use priority levels to identify what's truly important
- Focus on 3-5 high-priority tasks per day

### Supporting Creative Flow
- Use the Idea Inventory to quickly capture inspirations
- Try random prompts when you feel stuck
- Don't worry about organizing ideas perfectly - just capture them

### Building Consistency
- Check in with ADHD Quest at the same times each day
- Celebrate completing quests, no matter how small
- Use the Time Log to track your progress over time

## Keyboard Shortcuts

- **Enter**: Submit forms
- **Tab**: Navigate between interactive elements
- **Space/Enter**: Toggle checkboxes and buttons

## Troubleshooting

### Data Not Saving
- Make sure your browser allows local storage
- Export your data regularly as a backup

### Timer Not Working
- Check if your browser tab is active (some browsers limit background tabs)
- Make sure your device's sound is on if you want to hear notifications

### Display Issues
- Try refreshing the page
- Adjust your browser zoom level if elements appear too large or small

## Getting Help

If you encounter any issues with ADHD Quest, please:
